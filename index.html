About Me

Karen Wright is a private piano teacher and accompanist in Portland, Oregon who emphasizes the importance of learning in a natural, language-based way and understanding the cultural and historical context of musical works. She is finishing work on her M.A. in Piano at Portland State University, where she serves as the piano graduate teaching assistant, teaching and assisting piano classes at the university. She received her B.S. in Piano Performance in 2009 from Corban University, where she distinguished herself as a vocal accompanist and academic achiever, obtaining the academic award at graduation from the Corban University music department. A member of the Portland Chapter of OMTA, she has 15 years of experience teaching students of all ages, from beginner to early advanced levels.

Lessons

Karen also has significant experience accompanying college-level vocalists and instrumentalists from junior high through college level. She has accompanied students in university student recitals, high school choir performances, District and State Solo and Ensembles in both the Salem-Keizer and Portland school districts. She recently collaborated with Hailey McAvoy, a vocalist from Eastman School of Music, in a special program of art songs by Pacific Northwest composer, Hubbard Miller.
